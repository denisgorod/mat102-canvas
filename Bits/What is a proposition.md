>[!d] Definition
> A **logical proposition** is a statement which either evaluates to true or false.

Examples: "$P_1$: Your mother has green hair" is a proposition (probably false). "$P_2$: At least 10 people will pass MAT102 this semester" is a proposition. "$P_3$: If $x$ and $y$ are real numbers and $x < y$, then $x^2 < y^2$" is a proposition. Note that propositions do not need to be *true* — they just must be unambiguously either true or false. "How many shirts do you own?" is neither a proposition nor a predicate, because it cannot evaluate to true or false. The word "proposition" is used both for mathematical results and for this logical type — context will make the meaning clear.

>[!idea]
> A proposition is any statement with a definite truth value—true or false, but not both, not neither, and not indeterminate.
