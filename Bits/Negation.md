>[!d] Definition
> The **negation** (NOT), written $\neg P$, flips the truth value: $\neg P$ is true when $P$ is false, and false when $P$ is true.

Negation is the simplest logical connective — it acts on a single proposition. If $P$ is "it is raining," then $\neg P$ is "it is not raining." If $P$ is true, $\neg P$ is false; if $P$ is false, $\neg P$ is true.

>[!idea]
> Negation flips truth: what was true becomes false, and what was false becomes true.
