In everyday English, "or" usually means exclusive-or: "I'll eat pizza or Thai food" implies not both. In mathematics, $\vee$ is **inclusive** — $P \vee Q$ is true even when both $P$ and $Q$ are true. If someone says "I'll eat pizza or Thai," the mathematical interpretation includes the case where they order both. Always use the mathematical (inclusive) definition when doing logic.

>[!idea]
> In mathematics, "or" is inclusive: $P \vee Q$ is true when $P$ is true, when $Q$ is true, or when both are true.
