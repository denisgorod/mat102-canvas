For each homomorphism below, determine and prove what its kernel is.

(a) $\delta: (\mathbb{Z}, +) \to (\mathbb{Z}_6, +)$ defined by $\delta(n) = [2n]_6$

Verify that $\delta$ is a homomorphism. Then find $\ker(\delta)$ and describe it as a subgroup of $\mathbb{Z}$.

(b) $\rho: (\mathbb{Z}_{12}, +) \to (\mathbb{Z}_4, +)$ defined by $\rho([n]_{12}) = [n]_4$

Verify that $\rho$ is well-defined and a homomorphism. Then find $\ker(\rho)$.

(c) $\tau: (D_4, \circ) \to (\mathbb{Z}_2, +)$ defined by:
- $\tau(r^k) = [0]_2$ for all rotations $r^k$ (including the identity)
- $\tau(s) = [1]_2$ for any reflection $s$

Verify that $\tau$ is a homomorphism. Then find $\ker(\tau)$ and describe which symmetries of the square are in the kernel.

For each kernel you find, verify that it is indeed a subgroup of the domain.

>[!question] Exercise
> For each of the three homomorphisms, determine the kernel and prove your answer.
