We know that if $f$ and $g$ are both surjective, then $f \circ g$ is surjective. But what if we only know the composition is surjective?

>[!question] Exercise
> Let $f: B \rightarrow C$ and $g: A \rightarrow B$ be functions such that $f \circ g$ is surjective.
> 1. Must $g$ be surjective? Prove or give a counterexample.
> 2. Must $f$ be surjective? Prove or give a counterexample.
