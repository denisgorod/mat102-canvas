For each map below, determine whether it is a group homomorphism. If yes, prove it. If no, find a counterexample (two elements whose image under the operation does not match the image of their elements under the operation).

(a) $\varphi: (\mathbb{R}^*, \cdot) \to (\mathbb{R}^*, \cdot)$ defined by $\varphi(x) = x^2$

(b) $\psi: (\mathbb{Z}_4, +) \to (\mathbb{Z}_4, +)$ defined by $\psi([n]) = [n+1]$

(c) $\chi: (\mathbb{Z}, +) \to (D_4, \circ)$ defined by $\chi(n) = r^n$ (where $r$ is the 90-degree rotation in $D_4$)

(d) $\beta: (S_3, \circ) \to (\mathbb{Z}_3, +)$ defined by $\beta(\sigma) = \text{(number of fixed points of } \sigma \text{)} \pmod{3}$

(e) $\gamma: (S_3, \circ) \to (S_3, \circ)$ defined by $\gamma(\sigma) = (12) \circ \sigma$ (left multiplication by the transposition $(12)$)

For each one that is a homomorphism, also determine its kernel and image.

>[!question] Exercise
> For each of the five maps, determine whether it is a homomorphism, and find a proof or counterexample.
