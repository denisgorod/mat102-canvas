In calculus, a function $f$ is (everywhere) continuous if
$$\forall c \in \mathbb{R},\, \exists L \in \mathbb{R},\, \forall \epsilon > 0,\, \exists \delta > 0,\, \forall x \in \mathbb{R},\, |x - c| < \delta \Longrightarrow |f(x) - L| < \epsilon.$$

>[!question] Exercise
> Negate this sentence to determine the mathematical statement that $f$ is *not* continuous. (Each quantifier flips and the implication becomes a conjunction.)
