Recall that $b \mid a$ means there exists $k \in \mathbb{Z}$ such that $a = bk$.

**Part 1:** For what values of $n$ is it the case that $n \mid 0$?

**Part 2:** For what values of $n$ is it the case that $0 \mid n$?

In each part, identify the complete set of values, and explain your reasoning by appealing to the definition of divisibility.

>[!question] Exercise
When does zero appear as a dividend, and when as a divisor?
