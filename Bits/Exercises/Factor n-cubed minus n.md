Show that $3 \mid (n^3 - n)$ for any integer $n$.

**Hint:** The course notes give a proof by case analysis (considering $n \equiv 0, 1, 2 \pmod{3}$), but there is a much faster way. Try factoring $n^3 - n$.

Your proof should be a single sentence or two once you see the factorization. Think about what you can say about the product of consecutive integers.

>[!question] Exercise
What does the factorization reveal about divisibility that case analysis obscures?
