# Prove coprime divides product

Prove the following statement: **If $a \mid bc$ and $\gcd(a, b) = 1$, then $a \mid c$.**

Your proof may use Bézout's identity (the fact that there exist integers $x, y$ such that $ax + by = \gcd(a, b)$) or any property of the greatest common divisor you've established.

This is one of the most useful divisibility facts: when two numbers are coprime, a divisor of a product must divide one of the factors.

>[!question] Exercise
>Use Bézout and the assumption that $a \mid bc$ to deduce $a \mid c$.
