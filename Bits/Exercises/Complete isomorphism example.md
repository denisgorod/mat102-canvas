Example 9 sketched an isomorphism between $\mathbb{Z}_2$ and the cyclic subgroup $\langle [2]_4 \rangle$ of $\mathbb{Z}_4$. Now fill in all the details.

The cyclic subgroup $\langle [2]_4 \rangle$ is generated by $[2]_4$ in $\mathbb{Z}_4$. Write out its elements explicitly.

Define $\psi: \mathbb{Z}_2 \to \langle [2]_4 \rangle$ by $\psi([0]_2) = [0]_4$ and $\psi([1]_2) = [2]_4$.

Now verify:

(a) $\psi$ is a homomorphism: check that $\psi([a]_2 + [b]_2) = \psi([a]_2) + \psi([b]_2)$ for all $[a]_2, [b]_2 \in \mathbb{Z}_2$

(b) $\psi$ is injective: show that $\ker(\psi) = \{[0]_2\}$

(c) $\psi$ is surjective: show that every element of $\langle [2]_4 \rangle$ is in the image of $\psi$

(d) Conclude: $\mathbb{Z}_2 \cong \langle [2]_4 \rangle$

What is the order of $[2]_4$ in $\mathbb{Z}_4$? How does this relate to the order of the group $\langle [2]_4 \rangle$?

>[!question] Exercise
> Complete the proof that $\psi: \mathbb{Z}_2 \to \langle [2]_4 \rangle$ is an isomorphism by verifying all three properties.
