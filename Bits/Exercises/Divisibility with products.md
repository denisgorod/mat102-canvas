Prove both of the following.

**(a) Cancellation in products:** If $a, b, c \in \mathbb{Z}$ with $c \neq 0$, and $ac \mid bc$, then $a \mid b$.

**(b) Divisibility of products:** If $a, b, c, d \in \mathbb{Z}$, $a \mid b$, and $c \mid d$, then $ac \mid bd$.

Both results follow from the definition of divisibility and Proposition 3(ii). Use the notation $ac \mid bc$ to mean "$ac$ divides $bc$", and appeal to divisibility properties as needed.
