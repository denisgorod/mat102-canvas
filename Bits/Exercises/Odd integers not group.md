The integers $\mathbb{Z}$ form a group under addition. What about the odd integers?

Let $O = \{\ldots, -3, -1, 1, 3, 5, \ldots\}$ be the set of all odd integers. Test whether $(O, +)$ is a group. You don't need to check all three axioms — often one fails immediately and you can stop.

Try adding two odd integers and see what you get. Then think about the identity element: is 0 in $O$? What about inverses?

>[!question] Exercise
> Show that the odd integers do not form a group under addition. Which axiom(s) fail?
