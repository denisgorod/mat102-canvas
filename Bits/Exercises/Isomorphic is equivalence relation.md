You've defined what it means for two groups to be isomorphic: there exists a bijective homomorphism between them. Now show that this is a reasonable equivalence relation on the class of all groups.

Show that the relation "$G \cong H$" (meaning "$G$ is isomorphic to $H$") is an equivalence relation. That is, prove:

(a) **Reflexive**: $G \cong G$ for any group $G$

(b) **Symmetric**: if $G \cong H$, then $H \cong G$

(c) **Transitive**: if $G \cong H$ and $H \cong K$, then $G \cong K$

Hints: For reflexivity, consider the identity map. For symmetry, you already know that the inverse of a bijection is a bijection — does it preserve the group operation? For transitivity, think about composing two isomorphisms.

>[!question] Exercise
> Prove that the isomorphism relation is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive on the class of groups.
