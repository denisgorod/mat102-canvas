Two bowls each contain the same number of marbles. Two players alternate turns. On each turn, a player removes any positive number of marbles from exactly one bowl. The player who removes the last marble (from either bowl) wins.

Show by strong induction that the second player always has a winning strategy.

**Strategy hint**: Think about symmetry. If after the first player's move the two bowls no longer have equal marbles, what can the second player do to restore symmetry?

>[!question] Exercise
>Prove by strong induction that if two bowls start with equal marbles, the second player has a winning strategy.
