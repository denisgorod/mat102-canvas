We can combine two functions by pairing their outputs: form a "product" function from a domain into the product of two codomains.

>[!question] Exercise
> Let $f, g: A \rightarrow B$ be functions, and define $h: A \rightarrow B \times B$ by $h(a) = (f(a), g(a))$.
> 1. Show that if $f$ or $g$ is injective, then $h$ is injective.
> 2. Is the converse true? That is, if $h$ is injective, must at least one of $f$ or $g$ be injective?
