Here are some additional functions to classify. Some require more careful analysis.

>[!question] Exercise
> For each function below, determine whether it is injective, surjective, both (bijective), or neither. Justify your answer.
> 1. $f_1: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}, \quad f_1(x) = |x|$ (absolute value)
> 2. $f_2: \mathbb{Z}^{+} \times \mathbb{Z}^{+} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}^{+}, \quad f_2(x,y) = \min(x, y)$ (minimum)
> 3. $f_3: \mathbb{Z}^2 \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}^2, \quad f_3(x,y) = (xy, y^3)$
> 4. $f_4: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow [0,1], \quad f_4(x) = \begin{cases} 1/b & \text{if } x \in \mathbb{Q}, x = a/b \text{ in lowest terms with } b > 0 \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$ (Thomae's function)
