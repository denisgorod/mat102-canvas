Prove both corollaries to Bézout's Identity.

**Corollary 1:** If $a, b \in \mathbb{Z}$ and $c$ is a common divisor of $a$ and $b$, then $c \mid \gcd(a, b)$.

**Corollary 2:** If $a, b \in \mathbb{Z}$, the integer $d > 0$ is a common divisor of both $a$ and $b$, and there exist $m, n \in \mathbb{Z}$ such that $am + bn = d$, then $d = \gcd(a, b)$.

These results follow nearly "for free" from the proof of Bézout's Identity, but proving them explicitly strengthens your understanding of why Bézout's Identity gives us complete information about the gcd.
