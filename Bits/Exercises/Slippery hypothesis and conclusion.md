Here are four statements about slippery numbers. (Recall: a positive integer $b$ is slippery if $3 \mid b$ but $2 \nmid b$.)

>[!question] Exercise
> For each of the following statements, identify the hypothesis and conclusion.
> - If $b$ is a slippery number, then $2b$ is not slippery.
> - If $b$ is a slippery number, then $b^2$ is also slippery.
> - The sum of two slippery numbers is a slippery number.
> - If $b_1$ and $b_2$ are slippery numbers, then $6 \mid (b_1 + b_2)$.
