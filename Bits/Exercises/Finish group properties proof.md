You've seen some basic properties that follow from the group axioms alone. Now it's your turn to prove some of them.

The full proposition states:

*(1)* The identity element is unique.

*(2)* For each $a \in G$, the inverse $a^{-1}$ is unique.

*(3)* Left and right cancellation hold: if $ab = ac$, then $b = c$ (and similarly on the right).

*(4)* The inverse of a product is $(ab)^{-1} = b^{-1}a^{-1}$.

You may have seen a proof of uniqueness of the identity and inverse already. For this exercise, prove the remaining parts: *(2)* uniqueness of the inverse, *(3)* cancellation (both sides), and *(4)* the inverse-of-product formula. In each case, use only the three group axioms.

>[!question] Exercise
> Prove the following: (2) uniqueness of the inverse for each element, (3) left and right cancellation, and (4) $(ab)^{-1} = b^{-1}a^{-1}$.
