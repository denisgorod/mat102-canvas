The odd numbers are arranged in a pyramid:
- Row 1: $1$
- Row 2: $3, 5$
- Row 3: $7, 9, 11$
- Row 4: $13, 15, 17, 19$

Find a formula for the sum of all numbers in the $n$-th row and prove it by induction.

**Observation**: Row $n$ contains $n$ consecutive odd numbers. The first number in row $n$ starts where row $n-1$ ended. Compute the sum of the first few rows to spot the pattern, then prove your formula by induction.

>[!question] Exercise
>Find and prove by induction a formula for the sum of all numbers in the $n$-th row of the odd-number pyramid.
