You've learned that the dihedral group $D_n$ is generated by a rotation $r$ and a reflection $s$. One key feature of this group is that it is *nonabelian* — the order of operations matters.

For $D_3$ (the symmetries of an equilateral triangle), verify directly that $sr \neq rs$. Draw a triangle, label its vertices, and track what happens when you apply $r$ (a $120°$ rotation) then $s$ (a reflection), versus applying them in the opposite order.

Now generalize: prove that $sr \neq rs$ in $D_n$ for all $n \geq 3$. Think about how $r$ affects the axis of the reflection $s$.

>[!question] Exercise
> Prove that $sr \neq rs$ in $D_3$. Then prove the result holds for $D_n$ with $n \geq 3$ in general.
