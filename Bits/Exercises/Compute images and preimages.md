Images and preimages can be computed for various types of functions, including those on higher-dimensional spaces.

>[!question] Exercise
> 1. Let $f: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ be given by $f(x) = \sin(x)$. Find $f([-2\pi, 2\pi])$ and $f^{-1}(\{0\})$.
> 2. Let $g: \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}$ be given by $g(x,y) = xy$. Let $U = \{(x,y) \in \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z} : x^2 + y^2 \leq 4\}$ and let $\mathbb{P}$ denote the set of prime numbers. Find $g(U)$ and $g^{-1}(\mathbb{P})$.
> 3. Let $h: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^2$ be given by $h(x) = (x^3, x^2)$. Find $h^{-1}([-1,1]^2)$.
