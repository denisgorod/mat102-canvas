We know that if $f$ and $g$ are both injective, then $f \circ g$ is injective. But what if we only know the composition is injective?

>[!question] Exercise
> Let $f: B \rightarrow C$ and $g: A \rightarrow B$ be functions such that $f \circ g$ is injective.
> 1. Must $g$ be injective? Prove or give a counterexample.
> 2. Must $f$ be injective? Prove or give a counterexample.
