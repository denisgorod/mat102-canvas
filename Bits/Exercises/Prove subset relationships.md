To prove $A \subseteq B$, you need to show that every element of $A$ is also an element of $B$. This is your first real proof — take an arbitrary element of $A$ and show it must belong to $B$.

>[!question] Exercise
> Consider the sets
> $$A = \{n \in \mathbb{Z} : n = 8x + 3y,\; x, y \in \mathbb{Z}\}, \quad B = \{n \in \mathbb{Z} : n = 5x + 3y,\; x, y \in \mathbb{Z}\}.$$
> Show that $A \subseteq B$. Is $B \subseteq A$?
