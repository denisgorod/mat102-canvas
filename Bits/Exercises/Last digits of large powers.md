Find the last digit (i.e., compute modulo 10) of each number:

(a) $7^{100}$

(b) $3^{1111}$

(c) $4^{223}$

(d) $8^{400}$

**Strategy:** Reduce the base modulo 10, then use repeated squaring or find a pattern in the powers. For instance, you might note that $7^4 \equiv 1 \pmod{10}$ and use that to simplify $7^{100}$.

Show your work, including any patterns or reductions you find along the way.

>[!question] Exercise
Why is it helpful to look for repeating patterns in the powers of a base modulo 10?
