A cyclic group is one generated by a single element $g$. Every element can be written as some power of $g$.

Now, does being cyclic imply commutativity? Take two elements in a cyclic group: they have the form $g^a$ and $g^b$ for some integers $a$ and $b$. What is $g^a \cdot g^b$? What is $g^b \cdot g^a$? Are they equal?

Verify this concretely in $\mathbb{Z}_6$ under addition. This is cyclic, generated by 1. Check that $3 + 4 = 4 + 3$.

Now write a general proof: show that every cyclic group is abelian. Your proof should use only the definition of a cyclic group and basic properties of exponents.

>[!question] Exercise
> Show that every cyclic group is abelian.
