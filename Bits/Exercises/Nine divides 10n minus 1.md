Prove that $9 \mid (10^n - 1)$ for all positive integers $n$.

**Hint:** Work modulo 9. Observe that $10 \equiv 1 \pmod{9}$, so what can you say about $10^n \pmod{9}$? Use properties of congruences or induction if you prefer.

Verify your reasoning on small cases: does $9 \mid (10 - 1)$? Does $9 \mid (100 - 1)$? Does $9 \mid (1000 - 1)$?

>[!question] Exercise
How does the fact that $10 \equiv 1 \pmod{9}$ immediately imply the divisibility?
