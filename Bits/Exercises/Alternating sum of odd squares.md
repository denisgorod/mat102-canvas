Find an explicit formula for $$\sum_{k=1}^{n} (-1)^k (2k-1)^2$$ and prove it by induction.

**Discovery phase**: First compute the sum for small values of $n$ (say $n=1,2,3,4,5$) to guess the pattern. Notice the alternating signs: $-1^2 + 3^2 - 5^2 + 7^2 - \cdots$.

Once you have guessed the formula, verify the base case and set up the induction step. Assume your formula for $n=m$, then compute the sum up to $n=m+1$ and verify it matches your formula.

>[!question] Exercise
>Find and prove by induction a formula for $\sum_{k=1}^{n} (-1)^k (2k-1)^2$.
