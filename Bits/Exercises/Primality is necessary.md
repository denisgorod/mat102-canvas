# Primality is necessary

**Show by example that the prime divisibility property fails for composite numbers.**

The property states: if $p \mid ab$ then $p \mid a$ or $p \mid b$.

Find concrete integers $p, a, b$ such that:
- $p$ is composite (not prime),
- $p \mid ab$ (p divides the product),
- but $p \nmid a$ (p does not divide a) AND $p \nmid b$ (p does not divide b).

This shows that primality is truly necessary for the divisibility property to hold.

>[!question] Exercise
>Choose a small composite number $p$ and factor it as a product; this hint may suggest what $a$ and $b$ should be.
