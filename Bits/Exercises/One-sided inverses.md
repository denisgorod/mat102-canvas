An inverse that works in only one direction is called a *one-sided inverse*. For each part, construct sets $A$ and $B$ and a function $f: A \to B$ satisfying the condition. Explicitly write down $f$ and the one-sided inverse $g$.

(a) Find $f: A \to B$ and $g: B \to A$ such that $f \circ g = \mathrm{id}_B$ but $g \circ f \neq \mathrm{id}_A$.

(b) Find $f: A \to B$ and $g: B \to A$ such that $g \circ f = \mathrm{id}_A$ but $f \circ g \neq \mathrm{id}_B$.

>[!question] Exercise
>In each case, describe which property ($f$ fails to have: injectivity or surjectivity) and explain why it forces the composition to fail in exactly that direction.
