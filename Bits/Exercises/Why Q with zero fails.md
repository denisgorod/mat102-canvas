You know that $\mathbb{Q}^* = \mathbb{Q} \setminus \{0\}$ is a group under multiplication. The nonzero rationals have a multiplicative identity (1) and every element has a multiplicative inverse.

But what if we tried to include 0 and make all of $\mathbb{Q}$ into a group under multiplication?

Try to verify the three group axioms for $(\mathbb{Q}, \times)$. Which one fails, and why? Be specific about which element(s) cause the problem.

> Explain what goes wrong if we try to make $\mathbb{Q}$ (including 0) into a group under multiplication. Which axiom fails?
