For each pair of sets, construct an explicit bijection and verify that it is both injective and surjective.

(a) Between the negative integers $\mathbb{Z}^- = \{\ldots, -3, -2, -1\}$ and the positive integers $\mathbb{Z}^+ = \{1, 2, 3, \ldots\}$.

(b) Between $\mathbb{Q}$ and $\mathbb{Q} \cup \{\pi, \sqrt{2}\}$.

(c) Between the dyadic rationals $D = \left\{ \frac{1}{2^n} : n \in \mathbb{Z}^+ \right\}$ and $\mathbb{Q}$.

(d) Between the half-open interval $[0, 1)$ and the open interval $(0, 1)$.

>[!question] Exercise
>You may invoke the existence of known bijections (for instance, between $(0, 1)$ and $\mathbb{R}$) rather than deriving them from scratch. Focus on constructing the bijection for your specific pair clearly.
