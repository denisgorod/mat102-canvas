(a) Generalize the result from the previous exercise: show that $|(a, b)| = |\mathbb{R}|$ for any real numbers $a < b$.

(b) What about the closed interval $[a, b]$? Does $|[a, b]| = |\mathbb{R}|$? Construct a bijection or explain using the Cantor–Schröder–Bernstein Theorem.

>[!question] Exercise
>For part (a), you may compose known bijections. For part (b), compare $[a, b]$ to $(a, b)$ or $(0, 1)$ using appropriate injections.
