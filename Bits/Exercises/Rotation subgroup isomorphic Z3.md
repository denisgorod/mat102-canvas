Recall the dihedral group $D_3$ (symmetries of an equilateral triangle), generated by a rotation $r$ and a reflection $s$. The rotation subgroup is $\langle r \rangle = \{e, r, r^2\}$, the three rotational symmetries.

Show that $\langle r \rangle \cong \mathbb{Z}_3$.

(a) Write out the group table for $\langle r \rangle$ under composition. (Remember: $r^3 = e$ and $r^2 \circ r = e$.)

(b) Define $\psi: \mathbb{Z}_3 \to \langle r \rangle$ by $\psi([k]_3) = r^k$. Verify that $\psi$ is a homomorphism.

(c) Show that $\psi$ is bijective (you need both injective and surjective).

(d) Why is $\langle r \rangle$ cyclic? How does that relate to the fact that it's isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_3$?

Reflect: The group $D_3$ itself is not isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_3$ — why not? (Hint: think about commutativity.)

>[!question] Exercise
> Prove that the rotation subgroup $\langle r \rangle$ of $D_3$ is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_3$.
