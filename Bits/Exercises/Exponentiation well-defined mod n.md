In modular arithmetic, we can add and multiply congruence classes. What about exponentiation?

Suppose $a \equiv b \pmod{n}$ and $c \equiv d \pmod{n}$. Does it follow that $a^c \equiv b^d \pmod{n}$?

Either prove the statement is true, or find a specific counterexample where the congruences hold for the bases and exponents but fail for the result. If it fails, can you find a weaker condition (on $n$, $c$, or $d$) under which the statement becomes true?
