The words "necessary" and "sufficient" describe the direction of an implication. If $P$ and $Q$ are predicates:

- "$P$ is **sufficient** for $Q$" means $P \Longrightarrow Q$ — having $P$ is enough to guarantee $Q$.
- "$P$ is **necessary** for $Q$" means $Q \Longrightarrow P$ — you can't have $Q$ without $P$.
- "$P$ is **necessary and sufficient** for $Q$" means $P \Longleftrightarrow Q$.

Example: "Being divisible by 4 is sufficient for being even" ($4 \mid n \Longrightarrow 2 \mid n$). "Being an integer is necessary for being a prime number" (prime $\Longrightarrow$ integer).

>[!idea]
> "Sufficient" means one direction of implication; "necessary" means the other; "necessary and sufficient" means both directions hold.
